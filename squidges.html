Squidgies

My role:

UX/UI designer


Overview:

Squidgies is a shopping baby app where by registering your kid it will just show the items that just to your registration. Eg: If you register a 2 year old boy it will show just boy items on size 2 years.


Problem:

A lot of times people enter a shopping app and find items they love but sadly they find it doesn’t have the right size or is out of stock. Most people see these as a waist of time or frustrating.


Pain points

After performing some interviews we realised these are some frustrations the user have when using shopping apps.
- Showing items not longer available.
- Taking too long going trough the filtering.
- Not too many options of payment.
- Not secure.
- Not reliable with the delivery (dates, items lost).
- Bad customer service.
- Technical problems (freezing, not allowing x services).
- Hard to understand.


User story:

A busy mum want to buy some clothes for her kid. She gets into the app, since she’s an existing user as soon as she opens the app she gets items that matches her kid size and gender. In the search bar she can look for whatever she’s looking for or she can brows through the items to see if she likes something. Its quick and easy because everything will be on his kid requirements.


Personas:



Solution:

By registering your kid specifications it will only show items that matches that registration, it will not show any item out of size or out os stock. If you see it in the app means it is for sure available for the kid you registered. It will be quick and effective and time saving since you don’t have to go through the filters or worry it will not be available for your kid size. The fact it is an aggregator shopping app allows you to shop form different shops in one app, so it is time saving as well.
